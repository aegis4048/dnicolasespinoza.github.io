Offshore total vertical stress

In an offshore case you have to take into account the presence of overlying water thickness . Again, we assume constant mass densities for simplicity. In the water, you cannot define a stress because there is no solid phase. Within the water zone, fluid pressure will increase according to Eq. 2.7. This pore pressure will continue increasing in the seafloor sediments as long as there is a connected pore network from the seafloor to depth . We will see later that stress is a “tensor” (with numerical values that depend on the orientation of the coordinate system) and that pore pressure affects effective stresses in the normal directions.
