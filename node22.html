In-situ measurement

The only way to know the current magnitude (and direction) of the minimum horizontal stress (if ) is to measure it. Subsurface measurements of are based in hydraulic fracturing methods (to be seen in detail later on in the course). Three types of hydraulic fracture tests include:

  • Extended leak-off test: performed during drilling with drilling mud. The minimum principal stress is measured at about the value of the fracture closure pressure .
  • Mini-frac test: Performed for completion purposes before the main hydraulic fracture treatment with fracturing fluid. The analysis to obtain is based on the same principles of the extended leak-off test.
  • Step-rate test: Used for continuous fluid injection. The formation parting pressure (when a hydraulic fracture forms) is related but higher than .
