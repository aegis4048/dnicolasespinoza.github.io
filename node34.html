Continuum mechanics solution of an arbitrary problem

As highlighted before, notice that there are 6 unknowns (9 unknowns if displacements are included) and 3 equations in Cauchy's equations of equilibrium (Eq. 3.2). The solution of a general problem with arbitrary boundary conditions requires more equations to have a determined problem (as many equations as unknowns). The solution of such problem requires knowledge of the material properties. We need equations that relate displacement to stresses. These equations divide in two:

  • kinematic equations, that relate displacements to strains, and
  • constitutive equations, that relate strains to stresses.
